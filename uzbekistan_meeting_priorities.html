Project 02

Naganman Airport PPP

Proceed to DD — conditional
Key Considerations
  • 1
    Mid-tier LCC airportServes the Fergana Valley (~12M population, ~⅓ of Uzbekistan) and has a structural reliance on air travel. The Fergana Valley is in the far eastern periphery of Uzbekistan, geographically opposite to Samarkand, which sits in the country's central-western corridor. Its traffic is expected to grow from ~0.75M to ~3.4M by 2045, supported by population growth and aviation expansion.
  • 2
    Highly fragmented competitive cluster (critical risk)Within ~100 km radius, Namangan directly overlaps with 3 competing airports: Fergana International Airport, Andijan Airport, and Osh Airport (Kyrgyzstan) — all serving the same catchment; notably, Osh is already the dominant regional hub (~1.2M passengers) and expanding to >5M capacity.
  • 3
    CAREC-driven consolidation risk (binary outcome)Central Asia Regional Economic Cooperation Program explicitly recommends avoiding fragmented, subscale aviation systems and moving toward one primary hub; under this logic, only one airport in the cluster remains. Unless Namangan airport is chosen as the regional hub airport in Fergana Valley, the model falls.
  • 4
    PPP structure with private upside~$150M private investment under a 20-year PPP (2-year build + 18-year operation), focused on terminal and commercial revenues, while government retains sovereign airside functions (runway, ATC), limiting infrastructure risk.
  • 5
    Returns conditional on hub designation and traffic diversion~20% IRR and ~7-year payback rely heavily on passenger growth and diversion from Osh, which is only achievable if Namangan is selected as the dominant hub; without this, the asset risks becoming subscale with capped growth.
